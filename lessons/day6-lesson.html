Lesson 6

The weight loss rate that actually works

One of the most common questions I get from new clients is: "How much weight can I expect to lose?"

It's a fair question. But it's not the right question to ask.

The better question is: how fast can I lose weight sustainably - while becoming a stronger version of myself in the process?

Because here's the truth. There are plenty of ways to lose weight fast. Crash diets, extreme restriction, hours of cardio every day. They work in the short term. But almost always, they're not sustainable - and they leave you weaker, not stronger.

The rate I recommend: 0.5 to 1% of your body weight per week

This range keeps fat loss moving without sacrificing the muscle you've built - or the muscle you're going to build.

If you're losing faster than 1% per week

When you cut too aggressively, your body doesn't just burn fat for fuel - it starts breaking down muscle too. That is definitely not a trade-off worth making.

The exceptions:

- If you are significantly overweight (more than 20 kg above your target), you may lose slightly more than 1% in the early weeks without much muscle loss. That's fine.

- If you're carrying a lot of water weight, you might see bigger drops early on. What you want to avoid is consistently losing more than 1% every week over 2-3 weeks.

If you're losing slower than 0.5% per week

This can feel demotivating - and if it's happening, it's worth looking at what you can adjust to pick up the pace slightly.

The exception:

- If you've started strength training, you're eating enough protein, and you're following a balanced diet, you might find you're losing inches but the scale isn't moving much.

In that situation you are actually in a better place.

You're building muscle. Muscle raises your metabolism. More muscle means you burn more calories at rest - which means the scale weight will come down eventually, and when it does, you'll be in a far stronger position than if you'd just lost weight quickly.

This phase usually doesn't last beyond 3-4 weeks. Flex that patience muscle - the scale will follow.

A note for women

Fat loss for men is relatively straightforward hormonally. For women, estrogen and progesterone shift throughout the month - and depending on where you are in your cycle, you may be holding onto extra water weight even when fat loss is actually happening.

Do not panic and do not slash your calories out of frustration. Extreme restriction doesn't last - and it almost always leads to bingeing, pulling you into that exhausting cycle of overeating and undereating. Wait it out. Your body is working even when the scale isn't moving.

Remember what we talked about on Day 3 - progress over perfection. This is exactly where that applies. The scale is one signal, not the whole story.

Speaking of which - the number you see on the scale each morning is probably not telling you the full truth anyway. Tomorrow I'll explain exactly why, and what to pay attention to instead.

Today's takeaway: Aim for 0.5 to 1% of your body weight lost per week. Fast enough to feel like there is progress. Slow enough to ensure that you don't lose muscle.
