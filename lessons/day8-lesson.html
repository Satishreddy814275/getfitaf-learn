Lesson 8

Vegetables to the rescue

Ok, so let's take another step forward towards building balance on our plate.

It's going to be extremely challenging to lose weight if you are battling hunger all the time. That's why you need foods that make you feel fuller.

And unfortunately, almost all the processed, "tasty" foods available in the market are designed to keep you wanting more and never fully satisfied.

Vegetables are the ideal choice on a fat loss journey.

  • They help you feel fuller because they are packed with fiber
  • They have very few calories - almost negligible compared to high-fat, high-sugar, processed foods
  • They are loaded with vitamins and minerals that help your body function at its best

Now, for the question - which vegetables should you be adding?

Most vegetables are low in calories, so you can add almost any of them in large quantities.

Just watch out for certain higher-calorie vegetables - potatoes, sweet potatoes, avocado, and corn. It's not that these are bad. It's just that they are higher in calories, so portion size matters more with these. We'll cover potatoes, sweet potatoes, and corn as carb sources, and avocado as a fat source in their own sections - so the portions we talk about here don't apply to those.

How much should you be eating?

Ideally, aim for at least one big bowl of vegetables in every meal. Think of a bowl that holds around 300 to 400ml of water - that's the size we're talking about, filled with vegetables.

You can also think of it in terms of fist-sized portions. One closed fist is roughly one portion. Aim for 1 to 2 of those per meal.

Quick visual guide

Make one closed fist. That surface area - your knuckles to the base of your fingers - is one fist-sized portion. Aim for 1 to 2 of these in every meal.

If that feels like too much right now, start smaller. Add one fist-sized portion to just one meal a day, and gradually build from there week by week until you're hitting 2 portions across all your meals.

Another way to think about it: try to load half your plate with vegetables at every meal.

Some people - like me - don't prefer vegetables for breakfast, and that's fine. Just make up for it with a bigger portion at lunch and dinner.

One more thing to note

Eat the rainbow. Vegetables of different colours give you different kinds of nutrients. Try to have a variety of colours across the week. It doesn't need to be perfect - even a little variety helps.

How should you eat them?

A lot of people assume "eating vegetables" means eating them raw. It doesn't.

You can have them raw, sauteed, roasted, in a curry, blended into a smoothie, or made into a soup. Whatever works best for you and is most convenient is the right answer.

One thing to keep in mind if you're making soups, smoothies, or curries: the vegetable portion - not the gravy or water - should equal about one bowl or two fists.

What if I just don't like vegetables?

When most people say this, they usually have one specific vegetable in mind.

For me it was bitter gourd 😅

But when I ask the same people to list the vegetables they actually enjoy - or at least can tolerate - I usually get a surprisingly long list. So take 3 to 5 minutes and answer these three questions:

Your vegetable audit

1. Vegetables you absolutely enjoy eating
2. Vegetables you don't enjoy, but don't mind
3. Vegetables you absolutely hate

Plan your meals around the answers from question 1 and question 2.

Another thing that can put your mind at ease:

Your taste buds adapt. The more you expose yourself to a food, the more tolerable - and eventually enjoyable - it becomes. I used to hate bitter gourd as a kid and avoided it for years. A couple of years ago I pushed myself to try it again - and it genuinely wasn't that bad.

So keep experimenting. Try vegetables you stopped eating as a kid, or ones you've never tried before. Your options expand every time you do.

Today's takeaway: Aim for 1 to 2 fist-sized portions of vegetables in every meal. Any vegetable works. Any cooking method works - as long as you use minimal oil. Start with what you enjoy and build from there.
