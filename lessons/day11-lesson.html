Lesson 11

How to deal with difficult exercises

There will be certain exercises in your program that feel genuinely challenging - not just physically, but in terms of balance, coordination, and stability.

Some common examples:

  • Reverse lunges
  • Romanian deadlifts
  • Bulgarian split squats

If any of these make you feel uncomfortable, or if you find yourself dreading them almost every session, there's usually a reason. Your strength might not quite be there yet for these movements. And with exercises like Bulgarian split squats and lunges, balance can be a frustrating obstacle if you haven't established it yet.

All the aspects you're struggling with - balance, single-leg strength, stability - these aren't areas to avoid. They're areas to work on.

These are the exact things that build overall strength and functional capability that translates into your everyday life.

So instead of avoiding these exercises, do a less intense version.

How to modify

For Bulgarian split squats specifically:

  • Set up beside a wall and hold it for balance
  • Hold a pole to your side - this gives you something to stabilise against
  • Reduce the weight, or do them with no weight at all
  • Hold a bench in front of you for support when pushing yourself back up

The same principle applies to the others - reduce the complexity first, then build from there.

How to think about it

✗ "I hate this exercise."
✓ "How can I master this?"
✓ "What's a version I can tolerate right now that will eventually get me there?"

Keep an open mind. Almost every client who has pushed through modified versions of these exercises has come out the other side feeling glad they did.

I can assure you - it is almost always worth it to explore and work towards mastering these movements.

One important exception: if you feel extreme discomfort - and more importantly, if there is any pain while doing these exercises - don't push through it. Talk to your coach.

Side note: drop your coach a message if you want an easier version of any exercise - we're happy to adjust.

Today's takeaway: Difficult exercises are usually the ones worth doing. Try a modified version, keep an open mind, and the goal is always progress - not perfection.
