Lesson 2

The 3-Workout Rule - here's what I used to do

One of the biggest things I see people struggle with when it comes to working out isn't motivation. It isn't time. It's consistency.

Today I want to give you a rule that has personally kept me on track for over 12 years - through busy seasons, stressful periods, and every obstacle life has thrown at me. I've been working out for 12 years and training clients for 7 of those. In all that time, this one rule is the reason I've never completely fallen off track.

I call it the 3-workout rule.

Miss 1 workout
I treated it as an anomaly. Life happens. I didn't think too much about it.
Miss 2 in a row
I stayed aware. I'd start thinking about how I was going to get back on track - but I wouldn't stress about it yet.
Miss 3 in a row - not happening
I would do whatever it took to work out that day. Sacrifice work if I had to. Sleep if I had to. Rearrange my entire schedule if I had to. But I would not let that third day slip.

I want you to adopt this same rule for yourself.

Here's why it matters.

Your fitness journey runs on momentum - and momentum is very easy to gain and very easy to lose.

Missing one workout? That's nothing. Missing two? Still completely recoverable. But missing three in a row is where the slide begins - where "I'll get back to it" quietly becomes "I'll get back to it next week." And we both know how that story usually ends.

This isn't just about workouts either. If you're eating outside your goals three days in a row - that's your signal too. Three consecutive setbacks, whether it's training or nutrition, is where you need to step in and do something about it before the momentum breaks completely.

Make this rule non-negotiable, and it will serve you for years.

One thing though - common sense always applies. If you're sick, injured, or genuinely unwell, rest. This rule is for life getting in the way, not your body telling you it needs recovery. Get better first, then get back on track.

Today's takeaway: Never let yourself miss three workouts - or three good nutrition days - in a row. That's the line between a small blip and a real backslide.
