Lesson 10

Is it okay to shuffle your exercises?

Another question I get a lot: "Is it okay if I reorder the sequence you've given me for my workouts?"

At GetFit AF, when I build your workouts, they are structured in a particular order for a reason. Ideally, stick to the sequence as much as you can.

But there's one situation where shuffling is completely fine - and that's a crowded gym.

The crowded gym problem

If the equipment you need isn't available, waiting around for a long time is a waste. In that situation, move on to the next exercise in your sequence and come back to the original one as soon as that equipment frees up.

The one thing I'd ask you not to do is take an exercise from the beginning of your workout and push it all the way to the end. The reason: workouts are typically built so that the more demanding, more technically complex exercises come first - when you're fresh. The exercises that don't require as much thinking or energy come later. Reversing that order works against you.

The rule

The skipped exercise doesn't get dropped - it just gets delayed. The moment that equipment is free, you go back to it, even if it means going slightly out of order.

The superset problem

Some workouts include supersets - two exercises done back to back with minimal to no rest in between. In a crowded gym, holding two stations at once is almost impossible. The moment you leave one, someone takes it 😂

In that situation, just do the two exercises one after the other as separate sets. You'll lose some of the intensity benefit, but you can still keep rest time short by moving quickly between them. That keeps you close enough to the intended stimulus without needing to camp out at two pieces of equipment.

The goal of a superset is to challenge the muscle with minimal rest. If you can't hold two stations, staying quick between separate sets is the next best thing.

Today's takeaway: Stick to the sequence when you can. In a crowded gym, it's fine to shuffle - just don't push complex exercises to the very end, and go back to any skipped exercise the moment that equipment is free.
