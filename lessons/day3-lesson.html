Lesson 3 — Rule 2

Commit to doing something on difficult days instead of letting go of everything

Let me explain what I mean.

One of the biggest mistakes I see people make in their fitness journey is swinging between two extremes.

Either completely all in
  • Cut out every favourite food, only eat what helps with weight loss (just salads and oats for me please 🥹)
  • Work out hard, all 7 days a week, no exceptions
Or doing absolutely nothing
  • Not paying any attention to nutrition, eating whatever comes their way
  • Not working out, not even moving out of their chair

And here's the trap most people fall into when life gets in the way. I call it the "why bother" mindset.

"I'm not going to be able to go to the gym 4 days this week - why bother going on the other days?"

"I have a lot of weddings to attend this month - why bother making adjustments to my diet now?"

You let go of everything you could do because you feel like not doing it the ideal way means it is not worth it.

Nothing could be further from the truth.

If you can't make it to the gym 4 days this week - what can you do with the other 3 days? Can you get a short home workout in on those 4 days you're missing? These questions only come up when you realise that your fitness journey doesn't need to be perfect. As long as you're doing the best you can with the circumstances in front of you, you will keep making progress.

So let go of the all-or-nothing mindset.

Embrace progress over perfection.

And the best way to do that is to ask yourself this one question every single day:

"What can I do to make a little bit of progress in my fitness journey today?"

Today's takeaway: A small workout beats no workout. A decent food day beats a complete write-off. Progress doesn't mean perfection - it just means you are consistently striving to do a little bit better.
